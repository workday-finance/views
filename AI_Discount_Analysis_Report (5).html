✓ Positive — Add-on Discount Discipline Improving
Add-on discounting fell from 66.2% in Q3 FY26 to 57.4% in Q1 FY27 on an ACV-weighted basis. ME is leading the improvement: ME Add-on dropped from 48.7% → 42.5%. AI-Only Add-on fell from 74.7% → 47.3% — a 27-point reduction, the strongest improvement signal in the dataset.
⚠ Watch — Net New Discounts Remain Persistently High
Net New remains highly elevated on an ACV-weighted basis — LE at 82.1% in Q1 FY27 (up from 80.5% in Q3). ME is better at 72.2% but also elevated. Overall Net New at 78.0% has barely moved across three quarters. At these levels, net revenue realization on new AI logos is heavily compressed and warrants a specific floor conversation.
↗ Insight — Flex Credits Pricing Tightened Significantly
The biggest positive surprise: deals below 5M credits (8¢ floor) saw avg price rise from 6.73¢ in 2H FY26 to 9.00¢ in Q1 FY27, with discount falling from 34.4% to 12.8%. The 5M+ tier (6¢ floor) now has 10 deals in 2H FY26 and 4 in Q1 FY27 — still directional but more usable. List price across all tiers is 10¢.

HiredScore Observation
The Old SKU has been remarkably stable at ~45–50% discount across FY25, FY26, and Q1 FY27 — the pricing motion here is established and predictable. The New SKU's commercial motion began in FY27; Q1 FY27's 48.2% across 18 deals is the true opening baseline. It's notable that it already lands close to Old SKU parity — the key question going forward is whether the New SKU can establish a premium above that level as adoption grows.
Evisort Observation
Evisort Old SKU improved dramatically — 56.2% (FY26, 478 deals) → 32.0% (Q1 FY27, 43 deals), a 24-point drop on a large, statistically reliable sample. This is the strongest pricing improvement signal in the dataset. The Evisort New SKU commercial motion started in FY27; Q1 FY27's 59.3% across 17 deals is its clean opening baseline. It currently runs ~27 points above the Old SKU's Q1 level, which warrants monitoring — though early deal counts mean this will likely normalize as volume increases.

Methodology & Data Notes
How this analysis was constructed, key assumptions, and known limitations

1Data Sources

  • AI SKUs tab — 9,911 rows covering FY25-Q1 through FY27-Q2; used for Sections 1, 2, and 4
  • Flex Credits Only tab — 544 rows, Q3 FY26 through Q2 FY27; used for Section 3
  • Source file: 05-29-26 AI Product Pricing.xlsx provided by Finance

2Discount Calculation

  • Formula: Discount % = 1 − SUM(ACV_USD) ÷ SUM(LIST_ACV_USD) — ACV-weighted aggregate per cohort (aligns with Joyce's methodology)
  • Uses Columns I (ACV) and J (List ACV) per YT guidance
  • Rows with LIST_ACV_USD = 0 or null excluded to prevent division errors
  • ACV-weighted: larger deals have proportionally more influence on the discount %. This reflects aggregate revenue impact rather than a simple average of deal-level discounts

3Exclusions Applied

  • Growth Event sub-type (OPPORTUNITY_SUB_TYPE = "Growth Event") excluded — per Joyce's methodology
  • Rows where LIST_ACV_USD = 0 or null excluded to prevent division errors
  • Rows where ACV_USD is null excluded
  • Renewals excluded from all sections
  • 285 rows excluded from AI SKUs (rows where ACV_USD is null — these are sub-period line items, not deal summaries); analysis is at deal level using NAME as the unique deal key
  • For Flex Credits: FLXCRD-C (complimentary product code) excluded entirely

4Segment Definitions

  • LE (Large Enterprise): Column D = "LE"
  • ME (Mid-Market): Column D = "ME"
  • AI-Only Add-on: Column A (AI_ONLY) = TRUE + Opportunity Type = Add-on Subscription
  • Mixed Add-on: Column A = FALSE + Add-on Subscription
  • Renewals excluded from all sections per standard reporting practice

5Flex Credits Methodology

  • QUANTITY and price summed across ALL contract year rows per deal (APTS_PERIODNUMBER__C not filtered) — total credits purchased = sum of all years in the contract
  • Price per credit = TOTAL_DISCOUNTED_PRICE_USD ÷ QUANTITY × 100 (in cents)
  • 2H FY26 = Q3 FY26 + Q4 FY26 combined per request framing
  • Volume tier cutoff: 5,000,000 credits — below = 8¢ floor; above = 6¢ floor; list price = 10¢

6Limitations & Caveats

  • Q2 FY27 deal counts are very low (12 Net New, 41 Add-on) — directional only, do not use for decisions
  • HiredScore New and Evisort New SKU FY26 deals excluded — new commercial motion began in FY27; FY26 appearances were old-motion deals
  • Discount averages are not ACV-weighted; a $10K and $10M deal count equally
  • Currency conversion for non-USD deals uses FX_RATE column; may not reflect current rates

Methodology · Analyst Script
Key judgment calls to know:
· ACV-weighted: larger deals have proportionally more influence on the discount %. This reflects aggregate revenue impact rather than a simple average of deal-level discounts
· Flex Credit volume tier: QUANTITY summed across all contract years per deal; split at 5M total credits (8¢ floor below / 6¢ floor above); list price 10¢
· 2H FY26 = Q3 + Q4 combined for Flex Credits section
· Q2 FY27 included in tables but flagged as partial / low-count
· New SKU (HS + Evisort) FY26 data excluded — old motion
· Renewals excluded from all sections
· Discount formula: ACV-weighted (1 − SUM(ACV)/SUM(LIST_ACV) per cohort), not a simple average — aligns to Joyce's methodology · 285 rows excluded where ACV_USD is null (sub-period line items with no ACV data) · Flex Credits tab NOT used in AI SKU sections — each tab analyzed independently
